# Navigate the Viewer

Mirador is built for close visual inspection. The basic rhythm is: open a resource, choose a view,
move through canvases, and use the side panels for context.

## Opening Content

Mirador may open with content already loaded or with an empty workspace. If you see an add-resource
control, use it to add a manifest or collection URL.

## Canvas Navigation

Use the available window controls to move between canvases. Depending on the manifest and available
view options, a window can present:

- **Single view** for one canvas at a time.
- **Book view** for paged material.
- **Gallery view** for scanning thumbnails.
- **Scroll view** for continuous reading.

## Zoom And Pan

Image canvases are displayed with deep zoom behavior when the underlying image service supports it.
Zoom controls, pointer gestures, and dragging let you inspect details without losing the broader
canvas context.

## Metadata And Attribution

Open the information or attribution sidebar panels to review descriptive metadata, provider details,
rights statements, and canvas-level information supplied by the IIIF resource.

## Table Of Contents

Some manifests include ranges for navigation. In Mirador, those ranges can appear as a table of
contents panel, which is especially useful for books, manuscripts, archival files, and curated
sequences.
